How it works

1. Buyer picks crypto
Buyer initiates payment (BTC/ETH/USDC) — invoice issued by licensed processor.
2. Custodial escrow
Crypto goes to a regulated custodian (Coinbase, BitGo, Fireblocks partner or lawyer multisig).
3. Legal + KYC
KYC + wallet provenance report provided to notary — contract locks EUR price at timestamp.
4. Convert & settle
Custodian or OTC partner converts to EUR and wires to seller's account; you provide receipts.

FAQ

Q: Will Bitotal hold my crypto?
A: No. We never hold client funds. Crypto is held by a regulated custodian or lawyer-led multisig during escrow.
Q: What if crypto price moves?
A: Contracts lock the EUR price at a defined timestamp (invoice time). Conversion is executed by the custodian/OTC partner to avoid volatility risk for the seller.
Q: Is it legal in Greece?
A: Yes — buyers and sellers can transact using crypto. Bitotal works with local lawyers to ensure compliance with KYC, AML and tax reporting.
Q: How fast is settlement?
A: From payment confirmation to EUR wired to seller: typically same-day to 48 hours depending on OTC partner and banking hours.
